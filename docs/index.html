Scope of Program

The following OWASP assets are in scope for the bug bounty program.

In Scope

  • Foundation Websites: owasp.org and all subdomains
  • Flagship Projects: OWASP Top 10, ZAP, Cheat Sheet Series, Dependency-Check, Amass, Core Rule Set
  • Project Websites: *.owasp.org project subdomains
  • Infrastructure: Docker Hub, GitHub organizations, CI/CD pipelines
  • Wiki & Documentation: wiki.owasp.org, GitHub wikis

Out of Scope

  • Third-party services (Meetup, Zoom, Google Workspace)
  • Social media accounts (Twitter, LinkedIn, YouTube)
  • Local chapter websites hosted externally
  • Denial of service or brute force attacks
  • Social engineering of OWASP staff or volunteers
  • Physical security testing
  • Clickjacking on pages with no sensitive actions
  • Missing HTTP security headers with no demonstrated exploitability

Special Focus

  • Authentication and session management flaws
  • Privilege escalation between regular and admin users
  • Stored XSS in wiki or project pages
  • SQL injection or NoSQL injection
  • Server-side request forgery (SSRF)
  • Insecure deserialization
  • Sensitive data exposure

Program Rules

Be a Good Citizen

Only test against your own accounts or accounts you have explicit permission to test. Do not access, modify, or delete data belonging to other users.

No Disruption

Do not perform any testing that could degrade or disrupt OWASP services. Automated scanning with high request rates is not permitted.

Confidentiality

Do not disclose the vulnerability publicly before it has been resolved. Allow reasonable time for remediation (typically 90 days).

Quality Reports

Provide clear, detailed reproduction steps. Include screenshots, proof-of-concept code, and impact assessment.

One Vulnerability at a Time

Report each vulnerability separately. Do not chain vulnerabilities unless the chain itself demonstrates a greater impact.

Legal Compliance

All testing must comply with applicable laws. OWASP will not pursue legal action against researchers who follow these rules.

Reward Tiers

Rewards are based on severity using CVSS v3.1 scoring.

Critical CVSS 9.0 - 10.0
$2,000 - $5,000
Examples:
  • Remote code execution
  • SQL injection with data exfiltration
  • Authentication bypass
  • Privilege escalation to admin
High CVSS 7.0 - 8.9
$750 - $2,000
Examples:
  • Stored XSS affecting multiple users
  • Server-side request forgery
  • Insecure deserialization
  • Significant sensitive data exposure
Medium CVSS 4.0 - 6.9
$250 - $750
Examples:
  • Cross-site request forgery
  • Reflected XSS
  • Open redirect with exploit
  • Insecure direct object reference
Low CVSS 0.1 - 3.9
$50 - $250
Examples:
  • Missing security headers
  • Information disclosure
  • Self-XSS
  • Minor configuration issues

Responsible Disclosure Process

Step 1: Report

Submit your vulnerability report through our secure submission form or email. Include all relevant details, proof of concept, and your assessment of the impact.

Step 2: Acknowledgment

Our security team will acknowledge receipt of your report within 3 business days and provide an initial assessment.

Step 3: Validation

Our team will validate and reproduce the reported vulnerability, and determine the severity and impact. This typically takes 5-10 business days.

Step 4: Remediation

Once confirmed, we will work to fix the vulnerability. We aim to resolve critical issues within 30 days and high issues within 60 days.

Step 5: Resolution & Reward

After the fix is deployed, we will notify you, issue the bounty payment, and coordinate on public disclosure timing if applicable.

Public Disclosure Policy

90-Day Disclosure

OWASP follows a 90-day responsible disclosure policy. After a fix is deployed, public disclosure can occur after 90 days from the initial report.

Coordinated Disclosure

We prefer coordinated disclosure. We will work with you to agree on an appropriate timeline for public disclosure.

Researcher Credit

With your permission, we will acknowledge your contribution on our security acknowledgments page and in any public disclosure.

Safe Harbor

OWASP will not pursue legal action against researchers who follow these guidelines in good faith.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can participate in the bug bounty program?

Anyone can participate, including security researchers, developers, students, and enthusiasts. OWASP volunteers and board members are not eligible for monetary rewards but are encouraged to report vulnerabilities.

How are rewards paid?

Rewards are paid via PayPal, wire transfer, or cryptocurrency (Bitcoin or Ethereum) within 30 days of the fix being deployed. For amounts over $1,000, we may require additional verification.

Can I submit multiple reports?

Yes, we encourage multiple submissions. Each unique vulnerability will be evaluated and rewarded separately. Duplicate reports for the same vulnerability will be rewarded to the first reporter only.

What if I found a vulnerability in a third-party OWASP project?

OWASP projects are maintained by individual project leaders. For flagship projects, we coordinate with the project team. For other projects, we will forward your report to the relevant project leader and facilitate communication.

What if my vulnerability is marked as duplicate or out of scope?

If your report is a duplicate, we will let you know. If it is out of scope but still valid, we may still acknowledge your contribution even without a monetary reward. We value all good-faith security research.

Do I need to create an account to submit a report?

No, you can submit a report through our web form or by emailing security@owasp.org. However, you will need to provide a valid email address for communication.

Can I use automated scanning tools?

Automated scanning is permitted at low intensity rates only. High-volume automated scanning that could degrade service performance is not allowed. Manual testing and targeted exploitation are preferred.

What OWASP projects are in scope?

All OWASP Foundation websites (*.owasp.org), flagship projects (OWASP Top 10, ZAP, Cheat Sheet Series, Dependency-Check, Amass, Core Rule Set), and OWASP infrastructure (GitHub repos, CI/CD, Docker Hub) are in scope.

How long does it take to get a response?

We aim to acknowledge reports within 3 business days, validate within 10 business days, and resolve critical vulnerabilities within 30 days. You will be kept informed of progress throughout the process.

Is there a Hall of Fame or recognition page?

Yes! With your permission, we maintain a Security Researchers Hall of Fame to publicly acknowledge contributors. You can choose to remain anonymous if you prefer.
